# Rules and Participation Terms

This page describes the rules of the challenge and the conditions of participation.

By joining the challenge and submitting results, participants are considered to have read and accepted these rules.

## Purpose of this page

This file is intended to state the practical and organizational rules of the benchmark. It can be used to clarify what is allowed, how submissions are evaluated, and what organizers may do in case of misuse, invalid submissions, or attempts to unfairly influence the leaderboard.

Participants are expected to review this page before submitting.

## General participation

Participation in this challenge is open to anyone interested in the task.

By participating, you agree to:
- follow the challenge rules
- submit only work that you are authorized to submit
- avoid any attempt to unfairly manipulate the benchmark or leaderboard
- respect the platform and other participants

## Submission rules

Submissions must follow the format and interface described in the challenge documentation.

In particular:
- participants must submit code in the required format
- submitted code must run within the resource constraints defined by the challenge
- submissions must produce valid outputs for the provided task

The organizers may reject or ignore submissions that are invalid, corrupted, incomplete, or not compliant with the expected interface.

## Fair use

Participants must not attempt to:
- exploit bugs in the platform or scoring process
- access hidden test data by unintended means
- interfere with the service or other participants
- submit malicious code
- artificially inflate scores without actually solving the task

Any submission judged to violate the spirit of the challenge may be disqualified.

## Evaluation and leaderboard

Submissions are evaluated automatically according to the metric described in the challenge pages.

The public leaderboard may reflect only part of the evaluation data. Final rankings, if applicable, may be based on additional hidden test data or on a final evaluation phase.

The organizers reserve the right to:
- recompute scores
- remove invalid submissions
- hide or reset leaderboard entries in case of technical issues
- update the evaluation procedure if needed to preserve fairness, while documenting significant changes

## Organizer rights

The organizers may modify, suspend, or terminate the challenge at any time, especially in case of technical problems, misuse, or fairness concerns.

They may also update these rules when necessary. Continued participation after such updates constitutes acceptance of the revised rules.

## Participant responsibility

Participants are responsible for their own submissions and for ensuring that their code and content comply with applicable laws and regulations.

Participants should avoid submitting confidential, sensitive, or unlawful content.

## No warranty

This challenge and its associated materials are provided as is, without guarantee of uninterrupted availability or error-free operation.

The organizers do not guarantee that the platform will always be available or that evaluations will always run without interruption.

## Contact

If you have any questions about the rules or believe an issue affects fairness or evaluation, please contact the organizers through the challenge discussion or contact channel.
